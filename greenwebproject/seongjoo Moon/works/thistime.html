<(this time) I'm literally lying, please believe me.>
Period : December 26, 2021 ~ January 10, 2022
Venue : Pado (198 Seongmisan-ro, Mapo-gu, Seoul)
Seongjoo Moon Solo Exhibition

In René Magritte's 1929 painting, <The Betrayal of Images (La trahison des images)>, the Belgian surrealist painter juxtaposes a picture of a pipe with the sentence "This is not a pipe (Ceci n'est pas une pipe)". Art has always relied to some extent on lies, such as saying "This is a pipe Ceci est une pipe" in relation to a picture of a pipe, and the audience accepts, believes, and agrees with this lie. It is through this process of believing the lie that art acquires meaning.

Art follows many of the same institutional frameworks as these lies. For example, animals are not allowed in art exhibitions, and the exhibition space is made up of white walls on all sides. A barrier is placed in front of the artwork to prevent people from touching it, and small text next to the artwork must contain the title of the work, the artist's name, and information about the work. Some of these institutional frameworks are clearly perceived, some are less clearly perceived, and some are not perceived at all.

This exhibition is an exhibition of lies. Some of these lies will be clearly perceived, others will hide themselves and may not be easily detected. And the viewer will be able to believe or doubt the lies they discover.
